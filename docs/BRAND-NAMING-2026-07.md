## The pattern that wins in Arabic B2B SaaS

Daftra, Qoyod, Salla, Zid, Rewaa: **one concrete Arabic noun from the customer's own working vocabulary**, 2 syllables, clean Latin form, no numerals (the Zr3i/Mozare3 "3"-style reads dated). Exact .com rarely owned — they live on .sa/.io/.app or prefixed .com; Arabic script in marketing, Latin on the domain.

## Recommendation: **غلة / Ghalla** (primary)

**غلة is the Egyptian farmer-owner's own word for the farm's money/yield/proceeds** — it collapses product, promise, and heritage into one 2-syllable noun, reads correctly in Gulf MSA (yield/revenue), and serves all three personas: owner (my غلة, visible), manager (the غلة is accounted for — his alibi), accountant (revenue). Tagline writes itself: **«غلتك تحت عينك — من أي مكان»**.
- Collision scan: workably clean — nearest neighbor is a Saudi coffee roaster (ghalla.store, different class); no agtech/SaaS collision found.
- Domain signals (UNVERIFIED): ghalla.com/.app taken; **ghalla.io (~$38)** and **elghalla.com (~$11)** showed available.
- Risks: Latin misread as "Gala"; run the KSA TM search vs the coffee brand before committing.

**Fallback: فدان / Feddan** — mechanically the cleanest (feddan.app + feddan.io available, zero collisions found, iconic Egyptian) but names the *land*, not the *control of money*; resonance drops in the Gulf (not their unit).

**Module name gift: عهدة / Ohda** — clean namespace, native corporate Arabic for custody/entrusted charge — the strongest pure-accountability word; ideal as the custody module's product name even if not the company brand.

## Trademark path (rough; verify with an agent)

- **Egypt (ITDA)**: file **class 42 (SaaS) AND class 9 (app)** — 42-only is a known gap; single-class filings so one opposition doesn't block the other; file **both Arabic script and Latin form**. Timeline 12–24 months (60-day opposition window); official fees low (hundreds EGP/class), agent all-in a few hundred USD/class. **Start now — it's the long pole.**
